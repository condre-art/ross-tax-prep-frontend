IRS Audit Defense Playbook

Ross Tax Prep & Bookkeeping LLC
Confidential | Internal Compliance Document

Purpose

This playbook defines mandatory procedures for responding to IRS notices, audits, and inquiries to protect the firm, its staff, and its clients.

Core Principles

  • Centralized communication only
  • Written responses only
  • No verbal admissions
  • Mandatory response buffer enforced

Notice Classification

All IRS notices must be logged, classified, and reviewed by Admin-level staff immediately upon receipt. Categories include:

  • CP2000: Income discrepancy notices
  • CP501-CP504: Balance due notices
  • Letter 525: Examination appointment
  • Letter 950: 30-day letter for proposed changes

Evidence Retention

All engagement letters, intake forms, DocuSign trails, filings, and IRS acknowledgments must be retained for a minimum of seven (7) years.

Staff Communication Rules

Staff are prohibited from speculating, admitting fault, or engaging in verbal communication with IRS representatives without authorization. All external communication must go through the compliance team.

Approved IRS Response Script

"All IRS correspondence is handled by our compliance team. Please submit requests in writing to our registered address. We will respond within the statutory timeframe."

Escalation Thresholds

Immediate escalation to Owner/Admin is required if:

  • Penalties exceed $10,000
  • Allegations of negligence or fraud arise
  • Criminal language is present in correspondence
  • Subpoena or summons is received

Post-Audit Review

Upon audit closure, conduct root-cause analysis, update SOPs, document findings, and implement preventive measures for future engagements.
